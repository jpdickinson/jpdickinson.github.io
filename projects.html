2021

NASA Artemis Mission - ARROW

Design Engineer | 2020-2021

With a team of 4 engineers, we designed and developed a prototype offloader for NASA's LSMS (Lunar Surface Mobility System) called ARROW. ARROW stands for "Automated and Ranged Relocation Of the LSMS for Wider application" and was designed to mount to the moon lander and is integral in allowing for successful offloading of other rovers and tools from the lander to the lunar surface autonomously. I was responsible for the design and development of the ARROW system, which included creating detailed CAD models, conducting simulations, and collaborating with cross-functional teams to ensure the system met all mission requirements and constraints.

Impact: Successfully designed and developed a prototype offloader for NASA's LSMS, contributing to the Artemis mission's goal of autonomous lunar surface operations.
